Thoughts

  • So far, it appears to me that the process of learning mathematics could be analogous to constructing a building: our instructors offer blueprints and scaffolding, but it is up to us, in our minds, to erect and furnish our own houses. Through sharing, we may welcome into our abodes our fellows; together, we may notice differences in our styles. I think from each person's specially styled brick, laid together, emerges our mansion of mathematics. In this section, I might record some ideas on the topic as they change with time, and in doing so, I hope I can contribute to sharing.

  • I think an independence from notation and sequence of presentation can indicate when an adequate understanding of an area is attained. That is, if things were to carry different notation, or characterisations and definitions swapped, there ought not to be much effect on the scholar — after all, the underlying theory remains unaffected. But, I still think there is something to be said for how a theory may be arranged for presentation. Understanding the choices behind different arrangements may not say anything about the content of the theory, but it may offer a view on the theory itself.
