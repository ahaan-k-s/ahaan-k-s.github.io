  • UCL Year 2 Research Project

    Some fundamental results about linear maps on normed spaces are presented. \(p\)-norms for \(0 \lt p \lt 1 \) are then looked at, leading to a study of so-called "quasinormed spaces" (the "norm" is allowed to satisfy a weaker triangle inequality).

  • Galois' Theory and the algebraic closedness of \(\mathbf{C}\)

    A proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra is presented, using ideas from Galois' Theory.

    Materials

    • Slides presented to professors, and classmates of the Galois' Theory class at UCL in December 2024.

    This was part of a group project. Our group decided to collect only the results necessary for proving the penulimate result for \(\mathbf{R}\). A few more results may allow the treatment in the more general context of ordered fields in which every positive element has a square root, and every odd-degree polynomial has a zero. This could lead to a study of the "algebraic closure" of a field.

  • UCL Year 1 Group Research Project

    We expore the "tiling" of various (mutilated) chessboards by dominos, looking to employ results from Graph Theory to give conditions on when such a tiling is possible.

  • Mathematics Pedagogy

    So far, it appears to me that the process of learning mathematics could be analagous to constructing a building: our instructors offer us blueprints and scaffolding, but it is up to us, in our minds, to erect and furnish our own houses of mathematics. Sharing with fellows could offer glimspes of each other's houses, and potentially some differences in appearance. In this section, I will record some ideas on the topic as they change with time, and in doing so, I hope I can promote sharing.
